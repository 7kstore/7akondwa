A man on a journey carrying a goat, hyena and a bag of corn comes across a river. He needs to get all his items across the river but he can't carry all them at once. If he starts with the bag of corn, then the hyena will eat the goat behind him, if he takes the hyena, the goat will eat the corn. The man can only carry one or two of them at once. What does he do?

Answer: The man takes the goat first, leaving the hyena with the bag of maize. He then goes back and takes the hyena across the river. He doesn't leave the hyena with the goat so he retakes the goat back to the other side. There he leaves the goat and grabs the bag of maize, crossing it to the required side. All items have crossed without a problem.

A man saw a fruit he wanted in a tree. The only thing preventing him from having the fruit was a monkey in the tree. What would this man need to do in order to obtain his fruit?

Answer: The man would fetch a nearby stone and throw it to the monkey. The monkey being irritated would in return grab the fruit and throw it toward the man and the man would have his fruit.
